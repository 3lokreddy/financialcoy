# Company Structure Analysis: 10 Categories — Employment & Scaling

This document profiles 10 categories of companies across different fields. For each category it lists representative companies, the human employment structure broken down by functional branch (department), and the scaling structure (how headcount expands as the firm grows from startup → mid-market → enterprise / multinational).

Headcount ratios are typical industry medians — they shift with automation, geography, and business model.

---

## 1. Technology — Software / SaaS

**Examples:** Microsoft, Google, Salesforce, Atlassian, Stripe.

### Branches (functional employment structure)

| Branch | Typical share of headcount | Core roles |
|---|---|---|
| Engineering / R&D | 35–45% | SWE, SRE, ML, QA, security |
| Product & Design | 5–10% | PM, UX, research |
| Sales & Marketing | 20–30% | AE, SDR, growth, demand gen |
| Customer Success / Support | 10–15% | CSM, technical support, onboarding |
| Operations (Finance/HR/Legal/IT) | 8–12% | G&A |
| Data & Analytics | 3–6% | DS, analytics eng |

### Scaling structure
- **Startup (1–50):** Flat. Founders own product + sales. Generalist engineers.
- **Growth (50–500):** Functional org. VP-level leaders per branch. Pods / squads form in engineering.
- **Scale-up (500–5,000):** Matrix: product lines × functions. Regional sales offices (NA, EMEA, APAC).
- **Enterprise (5,000+):** Business units (BUs) by product. Shared services centralized (platform eng, security). Engineering split into platform vs. product.

## 2. Financial Services — Banking

**Examples:** JPMorgan Chase, HSBC, Bank of America, Citi.

### Branches

| Branch | Share | Core roles |
|---|---|---|
| Retail / Consumer Banking | 30–40% | Tellers, branch managers, relationship managers |
| Corporate & Investment Banking | 10–15% | Bankers, traders, analysts |
| Wealth & Asset Mgmt | 5–10% | Advisors, portfolio managers |
| Risk, Compliance, Legal | 10–15% | AML, credit risk, regulatory |
| Technology & Operations | 20–25% | Back-office ops, IT, payments |
| Finance, HR, Corp Functions | 5–10% | G&A |

### Scaling structure
- **Single branch / regional:** Branch manager → tellers / loan officers. Centralized back office.
- **National:** Regional VPs over groups of branches. Separate divisions for retail, commercial, wealth.
- **Global / universal bank:** Geographic regions × lines of business matrix. 3 lines of defense (business / risk / audit). Hub cities for IB; back-office centers in low-cost jurisdictions (India, Poland, Philippines).

## 3. Healthcare — Hospital Systems

**Examples:** HCA, Mayo Clinic, NHS Trusts, Apollo Hospitals.

### Branches

| Branch | Share | Core roles |
|---|---|---|
| Clinical — Nursing | 25–35% | RNs, LPNs, nurse managers |
| Clinical — Physicians | 8–12% | Attendings, residents, specialists |
| Allied Health | 10–15% | Therapists, techs, pharmacists |
| Support Services | 15–20% | Housekeeping, food, transport |
| Administration | 8–12% | Billing, scheduling, HIM |
| IT / Biomed / Facilities | 5–8% | EHR, devices, plant ops |
| Finance / HR / Legal | 4–6% | G&A |

### Scaling structure
- **Single hospital:** CEO/COO/CMO/CNO triad. Department heads per service line (ER, ICU, surgery).
- **Regional system:** System CEO over hospital CEOs. Centralized revenue cycle, supply chain, IT.
- **National / IDN:** Service-line organization (cardiology, oncology) crosses facilities. Shared services centers for billing/coding. Physician groups as separate legal entities.

## 4. Manufacturing — Automotive / Industrial

**Examples:** Toyota, Volkswagen, GM, Siemens.

### Branches

| Branch | Share | Core roles |
|---|---|---|
| Production / Plant Operations | 50–60% | Line workers, technicians, supervisors |
| Engineering (Design + Mfg) | 10–15% | Mechanical, electrical, process |
| Supply Chain & Logistics | 5–10% | Procurement, planning, warehouse |
| Quality & Safety | 3–5% | QA inspectors, EHS |
| Sales & Aftermarket | 8–12% | Dealer mgmt, parts, service |
| R&D | 3–6% | New product, materials |
| Corporate / G&A | 4–7% | Finance, HR, IT |

### Scaling structure
- **Single plant:** Plant manager → shift supervisors → line leads → operators.
- **Multi-plant national:** VP Manufacturing over plant managers. Centralized engineering and procurement.
- **Global OEM:** Regional manufacturing (NA, EU, Asia) × vehicle/product platform. Tier-1 supplier ecosystem. Centers of excellence (powertrain, EV, software). Global HQ R&D + regional adaptation.

## 5. Retail — Brick-and-Mortar / Omnichannel

**Examples:** Walmart, Tesco, Carrefour, Target.

### Branches

| Branch | Share | Core roles |
|---|---|---|
| Store Operations | 70–80% | Cashiers, stockers, store managers |
| Distribution / Logistics | 8–12% | Warehouse, drivers, planners |
| Merchandising / Buying | 2–4% | Buyers, category mgrs, planners |
| Marketing & E-commerce | 2–4% | Digital, brand, CRM |
| Corporate / HQ | 3–5% | Finance, HR, IT, real estate |
| Loss Prevention & Security | 1–2% | LP officers, asset protection |

### Scaling structure
- **Single store:** Store manager → assistant managers → department leads → associates.
- **Regional chain:** District managers (10–20 stores each) → regional VPs.
- **National/Global:** Country presidents → regions → districts → stores. Central buying with regional override. DC network sized 1 per ~150 stores. Online fulfillment centers separate org.

## 6. Energy — Oil & Gas / Utilities

**Examples:** Shell, ExxonMobil, Chevron, NextEra, EDF.

### Branches

| Branch | Share | Core roles |
|---|---|---|
| Upstream (E&P) | 20–30% | Geologists, rig crew, petroleum eng |
| Midstream (Pipelines, LNG) | 5–10% | Operators, technicians |
| Downstream (Refining, Retail) | 25–35% | Refinery ops, station mgmt |
| Trading & Commercial | 2–4% | Traders, schedulers |
| HSE & Engineering | 8–12% | Safety, maintenance, projects |
| Corporate / Finance / Legal | 5–8% | G&A |
| New Energies / Renewables | 5–10% (growing) | Wind, solar, hydrogen |

### Scaling structure
- **Independent operator:** Functional org per asset (single field/refinery).
- **Integrated national:** Business segments (upstream/downstream) with shared services.
- **Supermajor:** Geographic asset clusters × business segment. Joint ventures common. Highly contractor-leveraged: 1 employee : 2–4 contractors at sites.

## 8. Consulting / Professional Services

**Examples:** McKinsey, Deloitte, Accenture, PwC.

### Branches

| Branch | Share | Core roles |
|---|---|---|
| Client-serving consultants | 75–85% | Analyst → Associate → Manager → Partner |
| Industry / Practice experts | 5–8% | Knowledge / domain specialists |
| Business services (Finance, HR, IT, Marketing) | 8–12% | G&A |
| Knowledge management / Research | 2–4% | Research analysts, librarians |

### Scaling structure
- **Pyramid / "up-or-out":** Tight ratios — e.g., 1 partner : 3–5 managers : 8–15 consultants : 15–30 analysts.
- **Boutique → Mid-market:** Functional practices (strategy, ops, digital).
- **Global firm:** Matrix of practice (industry × capability) × geography. Member-firm partnership in Big Four. Delivery centers offshore (India, Poland, Philippines) separate from on-site teams.

| Sales / Customer Service | 8–12% | Account mgmt, call center |

- **Regional carrier:** Hub-and-spoke; terminal managers, dispatch, fleet ops.
- **Global integrator:** Multi-modal (air, ground, ocean). Regional networks linked by intercontinental hubs (Memphis, Louisville, Leipzig, Hong Kong). Heavy contractor model in last-mile.

## Cross-Category Patterns

1. **Frontline-heavy industries** (Retail, Hospitality, Logistics, Manufacturing) carry 60–80% of staff in operating roles; corporate stays small.
2. **Knowledge-heavy industries** (SaaS, Consulting, Banking IB, Pharma R&D) invert: 30–50% in expert/professional roles, with pyramid leverage.
3. **Common scaling progression:**
   - Stage 1 — Functional (one of each role)
   - Stage 2 — Functional with VPs and middle management
   - Stage 3 — Divisional (by product or geography)
   - Stage 4 — Matrix (product × geography × function)
   - Stage 5 — Federated / holding (BUs with shared services + corporate center)
4. **Span of control** narrows from ~15:1 frontline (retail/hospitality) to ~5–7:1 in knowledge work.
5. **Shared services / GBS** (Finance, HR, IT, procurement) typically consolidate when a firm passes ~$1–2B revenue or 5,000 employees, often offshored.
6. **Contractor leverage** is highest in Energy, Logistics last-mile, Media production, and IT services — sometimes 1:2 to 1:4 vs. employees.

---

## Glossary

- **Span of control:** Number of direct reports per manager.
- **Matrix org:** Employees report to two axes (e.g., function + region).
- **Shared services / GBS:** Centralized back-office serving all BUs.
- **Pyramid leverage:** Ratio of junior to senior staff (key in consulting / law).
- **Asset-light:** Firm owns brand/IP, third parties own physical assets (common in hotels, fast food).
